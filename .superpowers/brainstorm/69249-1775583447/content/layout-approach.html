Timeline Dashboard Layout

Branded header, practical everything else

A

Single-Page Scroll

Branded header at top, then a single Gantt timeline with all 12 engagements (8 retainers + 4 projects) stacked vertically. RAG status, budget burn, and milestones all visible inline on each bar. Filter controls at top to toggle retainers vs. projects.

Pros

  • Everything on one screen
  • Easy to scan top-to-bottom
  • Simple mental model

Cons

  • Can get tall with 12 rows
  • Detail lives in tooltips/hovers
B

Timeline + Detail Panel

Branded header, then Gantt timeline on top (compact bars). Clicking a bar opens a detail panel below showing: budget breakdown, milestone checklist, team assignments, and health scores. Summary stats row between header and timeline.

Pros

  • Timeline stays compact and scannable
  • Rich detail on demand
  • Room for milestone lists and budget charts

Cons

  • Requires interaction to see details
  • Slightly more complex
C

Summary Cards + Timeline

Branded header, row of KPI summary cards (total clients, at-risk count, budget health, upcoming deadlines), then the Gantt timeline below. Each bar shows RAG dot + progress. Hover for quick detail, click for full breakdown in a modal.

Pros

  • KPI cards give instant pulse check
  • Timeline is the main view
  • Modals keep the layout clean

Cons

  • Modals can feel disruptive
  • More UI complexity
