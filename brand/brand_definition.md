# Sidebar Creative — Brand Definition

## Agency Overview

**Sidebar Creative** is a 40-person independent digital agency headquartered in Portland, Oregon's Central Eastside Industrial District. Founded in 2016 by four partners who met at Wieden+Kennedy, the agency has grown from a three-person design shop into a full-service creative studio generating **$6.5M in annual revenue**.

**Specializations:**
- Brand identity and visual systems
- Web design and custom development (primarily Shopify Plus, headless builds, and custom React/Next.js)
- Integrated marketing campaigns spanning paid media, organic social, email, and content

**Target clients:** Mid-market consumer brands doing $10M–$150M in annual revenue — companies that have outgrown freelancers but don't need (or can't afford) a holding-company agency. Sweet spot is DTC and retail brands in outdoor, food & beverage, beauty, home goods, and lifestyle categories.

**Revenue breakdown (FY2025):**
- Retainer revenue: $2.64M (40.6%)
- Project-based revenue: $3.16M (48.6%)
- Strategy/consulting engagements: $0.70M (10.8%)

## Operational Pain Points

The agency has grown faster than its operational infrastructure. The four founding partners still make most scoping decisions, and the systems that worked at 15 people are buckling at 40.

**Scope creep is endemic.** Projects consistently run 15–30% over their scoped hours. Account directors absorb client requests without formal change orders because "it's easier to just do it than have the conversation." The result: margins erode, and the team blames poor scoping when the real issue is untracked scope expansion.

**Resource loading is blind.** Some team members are working 50+ hour weeks while others have open capacity. There's no single view of who's assigned to what. Partners allocate people based on hallway conversations and gut feel. The senior designer and lead developer are on every major project because they're the "safe" choice, which burns them out while junior staff don't get growth opportunities.

**Status meetings consume PM time.** The six account/project managers spend a combined **8 hours per week** in internal status meetings, plus another 6 hours preparing for them. That's 14 hours of senior PM time per week spent on reporting instead of managing.

**Profitability is invisible.** The agency doesn't know which clients are profitable until the quarter ends and the bookkeeper reconciles time logs against invoices. Two retainer clients are almost certainly unprofitable, but nobody can prove it with current tools.

**Asana is a graveyard.** The team adopted Asana three years ago. It now contains 2,400+ open tasks across 45 projects, most of which are outdated. Nobody trusts the data. People track real work in personal notebooks, Slack threads, and spreadsheets. Every attempt to "clean up Asana" lasts about two weeks before entropy wins.

**Client reporting is a Friday time sink.** Account directors spend every Friday afternoon manually assembling client performance reports in Google Slides — pulling data from Google Analytics, social platforms, email tools, and the CRM. Each report takes 2–3 hours to build. With 8 active retainer clients, that's **16–24 hours of senior account time per month** spent on formatting slides instead of strategic thinking.

## Client Reporting Context

### The Friday Report Problem

Every Friday afternoon, the account team stops doing strategic work and starts assembling client performance reports in Google Slides. The process:

1. **Pull website data** from Google Analytics (15–20 min per client)
2. **Pull social metrics** from Meta Business Suite, LinkedIn, TikTok (10–15 min)
3. **Pull email data** from Klaviyo or Mailchimp (10 min)
4. **Pull CRM/pipeline data** from HubSpot (10 min)
5. **Pull hours/budget data** from Asana and Harvest (15 min — often inaccurate)
6. **Build the slide deck** — copy data into templates, add commentary, format charts (45–60 min)
7. **Review and send** (15 min)

**Total per client: 2–3 hours. Total per month across 8 clients: 16–24 hours.**

This is senior account director and account manager time — people billing at $175–$225/hour internally. The reporting process costs the agency an estimated **$3,500–$5,000/month** in unbillable senior time, and the reports are often outdated by the time clients read them Monday morning.

### Reporting Objective

**Dani Ferraro (Operations Director)** needs tools that:

1. **Eliminate the Friday report grind** — auto-generate or pre-populate client performance reports from connected data sources
2. **Provide real-time client health visibility** — a dashboard showing which clients are green/yellow/red based on performance metrics, profitability, and relationship signals
3. **Flag issues early** — automated alerts when a client's metrics trend downward for 2+ consecutive months, when hours burn past 80% of monthly allocation before the 3rd week, or when profitability drops below threshold
4. **Support quarterly business reviews** — historical trend data that account directors can use in QBR presentations without manual assembly
5. **Work with existing tools** — pull from GA4, Meta, Klaviyo, HubSpot, and Harvest without requiring the team to change their workflows

### Client Health Scoring

Client health is scored across three dimensions (see `data/client_health_framework.md` for full framework):

- **Performance** (40% weight): Are we hitting the client's KPIs?
- **Profitability** (35% weight): Is the account margin-positive after actual hours?
- **Relationship** (25% weight): NPS, responsiveness, escalation frequency, expansion signals

## Operational Objectives

**Owner:** Dani Ferraro, Operations Director
**Sponsor:** Maya Chen, Managing Partner
**Timeline:** Q2 2026

### Primary Objectives

1. **Real-time resource allocation visibility** — See who is working on what, at what capacity, across all active projects and retainers. Eliminate the "hallway conversation" staffing model.

2. **Project health dashboards** — Traffic-light status for every active engagement showing: hours burned vs. budgeted, milestone progress, scope change log, and margin trajectory.

3. **Team capacity planning** — Forward-looking view of team loading for the next 4–6 weeks so partners can make staffing decisions before people hit 140% utilization.

4. **Per-client profitability tracking** — Real-time (not quarterly) view of revenue vs. cost per client, including blended hourly rates, overage tracking, and margin alerts.

5. **Automated client reporting** — Replace the Friday slide deck assembly with auto-generated or pre-populated reports pulling from GA4, Meta, Klaviyo, HubSpot, and Harvest.

6. **Client health scoring** — Composite health scores updated monthly, combining performance metrics, profitability data, and relationship signals into a single dashboard view.

### Constraints

- **Don't replace Asana.** The team won't adopt another PM tool. Build on top of or alongside existing tools.
- **Don't require behavior change.** People already log time in Harvest and manage tasks (loosely) in Asana. Solutions should pull from existing data, not ask the team to enter data in new places.
- **Start with visibility, not automation.** The first win is showing accurate data. Workflow automation comes second.
- **Budget: $15K–$25K** for tooling and implementation in Q2 2026, plus ongoing Dani's time.
